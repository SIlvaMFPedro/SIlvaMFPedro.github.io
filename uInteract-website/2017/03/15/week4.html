First Code samples







Vertigo

This week the bulk of our work was directed to building a functional environment where we could experiment and estimate how well the work developed until now was going in the direction we truly want for our final state of the demonstration. The result of this can be seen in the short video below:


Current features include:

  • Avatar replicates user movements (to a certain extent)
  • Early version of the environment where the user will have to walk upon

From this moment on, focus will be directed into developing the hitboxes of the avatar in order to connect better with the board and extracting correct information from Kinect to apply into the Avatar to rectify some movement related issues.

Avatar

When it comes to the Avatar demo, after much discussion and experimentation we decided to develop our own models for the avatar using "BLENDER" so that we could manipulate directly its joints, in order to serve as the pillar of support for the Mesh that will be implemented later on (highlights of the movements by the user). Dust Under Investigation

Leap Motion's current version "Orion", saw the discontinuing of gesture based implementations, due to them being considered as not working the way they were originally designed for. Therefore, we have since coded some gesture based scripts that mainly reflect "Swipes" and "Grab" movements which we deemed necessary to implement in the prototype interface.


As it can be seen from the previous video, some optimizations are still needed, in regards to the velocity of gestures - It has to a way too fast movement - and also at the quantity of times that same gesture is detected, which is directly related to the frames.

About the interface, we've got some already "functional" images to show you, from a very early stage of development, where it can be seen how we intend to give actions to a user to follow a chart and interact with it
