What's it all about:

Our aim is to develop interactive and intuitive demonstrations, within the scope of exhibitions at the Center of Live Science Center of Aveiro (FCCV), that allow the use of Virtual Reality and recently acquired interaction equipment, such as kinect (v1) cameras, Leap Motion Controller and Virtual Reality Glasses (Oculus Rift DK2).

Why choose such a project?

The challenge involved, from the point of view of the demonstrations, is to carry out the study on how to handle, from the programmatic point of view, the various Virtual Reality components involved in the project, as well as to interconnect several of these in the same application To develop some stable and tested demonstrations that can be quickly installed in the FCCV. This should also guarantee effective functionalities for demonstration implementation in an intuitive way that is not heavy so that a user outside the programmatic context can develop them without any mishaps.

In this way, a lot of emphasis will be placed on documentation, support and robustness of the solutions so that there are no problems during development that can prevent good deployment within the defined objectives.

Vertigo - Defy your sense of height

With "Vertigo" you can face your fear of heights in a virtual space closer to reality, using kinect and Oculus Rift cameras, where you are on a raised plane in relation to the ground, and have to cross a given path to reach a certain destination. During this movement, there might be several events related to the breaking of the soil where your feet are near to, where you will have to move through strategic points so as not to fall from a high height, and in some cases the space where you are stepping through is short, which can induce a possible vertigo sensation.

Avatar - Learn about your body

With "Avatar" our aim is to allow you to control fully a 3D model of an avatar designed on a screen where your gestures detected by Kinect are replicated. When you move a certain part of your body, the muscles used in that movement will be highlighted on the virtual avatar, with specific information about each tissue involved.

Dust Under Investigation - Interact with a 3D interface

"Dust Under Investigation" is a demo being developed for an activity to be adapted for FCCV (Fábrica Centro Ciência Viva) or UA (Universidade de Aveiro) promotion in the context of a fair which goal is to create a configurable interactive gallery to allow users to see information about the experience, information that is crucial like a news in a newspaper and photos with information about suspects that are being investigated in this experience.
This application aims to interact with multiple photos, videos and menus in an interactive way using leap as a control, where some possible actions would be: Swipe, Click, Drag.

Although this is being developed to a specific scenario, it can be adapted to a general interactive multimedia interface in a vast range of scenarios.

Technical specifications

The Virtual Reality equipment to be used in the scope of this project are the following:

  • Oculus Rift DK2 (3x USB-3 Ports)
  • Leap Motion Controller (1x USB-3 Port)
  • Kinect v1 Camera (1x USB port)

The framework on which the demos should be based is the UNITY 5 open source development platform.

A Desktop equipped with a GPU capable of VR-READY is recommended.

Risks and Challenges

Escalability

This project can be characterized by its ability to scale rapidly, since there is always the possibility of adding new features to the developed statements as well as improving them, which may take longer than initially foreseen and which may result In delays in the development of the remaining objectives of the project, so it will be necessary to make a good forecast in a primordial state of the project of what is really intended in the final state of the demonstrations and framework and that it is possible to do within the established time.

Calibration

Since the demonstrations involved in the project consist in the use of different Virtual/Augmented Reality components, it is necessary to ensure that the work developed is usable when it comes to certain movements being recognized in the most correct way
